In this blog, I will talk about phase 1 JavaScript that I am currently studying at Academy-XI.

JavaScript is proving to be somewhat difficult for me to grasp compared to html and css, even though some say it is quite easy to learn. Having used and still use a lot of logic calculations and formulas in excel and tableau etc., I thought js would be relatively easy for me however I am finding it isn't. Having the ability to problem solve and create work arounds is imperative in the industry I work in and js seems like it would be a good fit so to speak.

To try and assist myself in learning js I have also searched the web for different ways to learn js but have still come up with very little in the way of simplified learning for this type of language. So, I have also looked into the way I am learning and studying and have found the following:

- Dwelling/digging into problems too much. Diving whead first into rabbit holes is my specialty and sometimes I don't realise I'm doing it.

- Studying for too long at one time with no break. As I live alone there is no one to remind me to take a break and I end up burning myself out a little.

- Trying to figure out problems on my own. I tend to want to figure out issues by myself instead of utilising the forums to ask questions and read what others have done and gone through.

To counteract all of the above I have had to set days and times in my calendar to ensure I take breaks and to set aside days for relaxation and spending time with my family. Juggling working full time and studying software engineering is tricky to say the least and does have an impact on those who are close to me due to the fact that I don't spend as muhch time with them and also looking quite tired most days.

Reading the above it all starts to sound a little depressing so I want to assure anyone reading this blog that's not the case. This is something I really enjoy doing and want to succeed in so I am not giving up, merely just explaining my JavaScript journey thus far.
